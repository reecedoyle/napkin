  prompt="(Taiwan IMO camp ★) In a town there are n people and k clubs. Each club has an odd number of members, and any two clubs have an even number of common members. Prove that k ≤ n."
  hint="Interpret clubs as vectors in 𝔽₂ⁿ and use dot products mod 2."
  solution="Represent each club as a vector in 𝔽₂ⁿ (the j-th coordinate is 1 if person j is in the club, 0 otherwise). Define a 'dot product' over 𝔽₂. The odd membership condition says each club-vector v has v·v = 1 (mod 2). The even common-membership condition says any two distinct club-vectors u, v satisfy u·v = 0 (mod 2). So the k club-vectors are orthonormal over 𝔽₂ and hence linearly independent. Therefore k ≤ dim 𝔽₂ⁿ = n."
