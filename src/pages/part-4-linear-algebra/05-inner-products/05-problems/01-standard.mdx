  hint="Fix an orthonormal basis e₁, …, eₙ. A Cauchy sequence in V gives Cauchy sequences of coordinates. Use the fact that ℝⁿ is complete."
  solution="Let V be a finite-dimensional inner product space and fix an orthonormal basis e₁, …, eₙ. Map each vector v = Σaᵢeᵢ to its coordinate vector (a₁, …, aₙ) ∈ ℝⁿ (or ℂⁿ). This map is an isometry: ‖v‖² = Σ|aᵢ|² = ‖(a₁,…,aₙ)‖². So a sequence in V is Cauchy iff its coordinate sequences are Cauchy in ℝ (or ℂ). Since ℝⁿ is complete, each Cauchy coordinate sequence converges, giving a limiting vector in V. Hence V is complete."
