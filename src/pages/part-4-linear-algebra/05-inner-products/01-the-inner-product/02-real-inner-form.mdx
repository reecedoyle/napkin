<SlideLayout title="Real inner forms">

For a real vector space we write $\langle v, w \rangle$ instead of $v \cdot w$.

<Callout kind="definition" title="Real inner form">
Let $V$ be a real vector space. A **real inner form** is a function $\langle \bullet, \bullet \rangle \colon V \times V \to \RR$ that is:

- **Symmetric**: $\langle v, w \rangle = \langle w, v \rangle$ for all $v, w \in V$.
- **Bilinear**: linear in each argument separately — $\langle cx + y, v \rangle = c\langle x, v \rangle + \langle y, v \rangle$ (and similarly in the second slot).
- **<Term k="positiveDefinite">Positive definite</Term>**: $\langle v, v \rangle \geq 0$, with equality only when $v = 0_V$.
</Callout>

<Callout kind="remark">
Symmetry plus linearity in the first argument automatically gives linearity in the second argument too — so we could have listed just one linearity axiom.
</Callout>
