<SlideLayout title="The norm">

The inner product gives us a notion of "length".

<Callout kind="definition" title="Norm">
Let $V$ be an <Term k="innerProductSpace">inner product space</Term>. The **<Term k="norm">norm</Term>** of $v \in V$ is

$$\norm{v} = \sqrt{\langle v, v \rangle}.$$

This is well-defined because <Term k="positiveDefinite">positive definiteness</Term> guarantees $\langle v, v \rangle \geq 0$.
</Callout>

<Callout kind="example" title="Norm on ℝⁿ and ℂⁿ">
For $v = (a_1, \dots, a_n) \in \RR^n$ with the standard dot product,
$$\norm{v} = \sqrt{a_1^2 + \dots + a_n^2},$$
which is exactly the Euclidean length. For $v \in \CC^n$, $\norm{v} = \sqrt{|z_1|^2 + \dots + |z_n|^2}$.
</Callout>
