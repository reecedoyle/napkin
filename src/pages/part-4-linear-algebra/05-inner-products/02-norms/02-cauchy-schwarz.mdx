The key lemma connecting norms and inner products:

<Callout kind="lemma" title="Cauchy-Schwarz">
Let $V$ be an inner product space. For any $v, w \in V$,

$$|\langle v, w \rangle| \leq \norm{v} \norm{w},$$

with equality if and only if $v$ and $w$ are linearly dependent.
</Callout>

The proof idea: use the <Term k="positiveDefinite">positive-definiteness</Term> axiom creatively by expanding $\langle cv - w, cv - w \rangle \geq 0$ for a cleverly chosen scalar $c$.

Using conjugate symmetry, this gives 2Re[c⟨v,w⟩] ≤ |c|²‖v‖² + ‖w‖². Choose c = (‖w‖/‖v‖)·(|⟨v,w⟩|/⟨v,w⟩). Then c⟨v,w⟩ = (‖w‖/‖v‖)|⟨v,w⟩| is real and positive, and |c| = ‖w‖/‖v‖. Substituting gives 2(‖w‖/‖v‖)|⟨v,w⟩| ≤ 2‖w‖², hence |⟨v,w⟩| ≤ ‖v‖‖w‖."
