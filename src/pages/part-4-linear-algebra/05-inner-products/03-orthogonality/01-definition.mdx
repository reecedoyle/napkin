<Callout kind="definition" title="Orthogonal vectors">
Two nonzero vectors $v$ and $w$ in an <Term k="innerProductSpace">inner product space</Term> are **<Term k="orthogonal">orthogonal</Term>** if $\langle v, w \rangle = 0$.
</Callout>

In $\RR^n$ this matches the geometric notion of perpendicularity: $v \cdot w = |v||w|\cos\theta$, so $v \cdot w = 0$ iff $\theta = 90°$. The abstract definition generalises this to any inner product space.
