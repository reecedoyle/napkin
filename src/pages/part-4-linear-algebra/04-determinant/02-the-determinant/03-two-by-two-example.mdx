<SlideLayout title="Determinant of a 2×2 matrix">

Let us verify the definition recovers the familiar formula.

<Callout kind="example" title="det of a 2×2 matrix">
Let $V = \mathbb{R}^2$ with basis $e_1, e_2$ and let

$$
T = \begin{bmatrix} a & c \\ b & d \end{bmatrix},
\quad T(e_1) = ae_1 + be_2, \quad T(e_2) = ce_1 + de_2.
$$

Then $\bigwedge^2(T)$ sends the basis element $e_1 \wedge e_2$ to

$$
T(e_1) \wedge T(e_2) = (ae_1 + be_2) \wedge (ce_1 + de_2) = (ad - bc)(e_1 \wedge e_2).
$$

So $\det T = ad - bc$.
</Callout>

The determinant is the unique scalar by which the map scales the single basis element of $\bigwedge^2(V)$.
