The <Term k="wedgeProduct">2-wedge product</Term> $\bigwedge^2(V)$ looks like a tensor product $V \otimes V$ with one extra relation.

<Callout kind="definition" title="2-Wedge product">
Let $V$ be a $k$-vector space. The space $\bigwedge^2(V)$ is generated by symbols $v \wedge w$ (for $v, w \in V$) subject to the bilinearity relations

$$
(v_1 + v_2) \wedge w = v_1 \wedge w + v_2 \wedge w, \quad
v \wedge (w_1 + w_2) = v \wedge w_1 + v \wedge w_2,
$$
$$
(cv) \wedge w = v \wedge (cw) = c(v \wedge w),
$$

**plus** the single extra relation $v \wedge v = 0$ for all $v \in V$.
</Callout>

The extra relation forces **anti-symmetry**: since $(v+w) \wedge (v+w) = 0$ expands to $v \wedge w + w \wedge v = 0$, we immediately get $v \wedge w = -(w \wedge v)$.
