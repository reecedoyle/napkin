<SlideLayout title="Finite vs. infinite dimensions">

Most Fourier examples in this chapter are finite-dimensional, but the star of the show — $L^2([-\pi, \pi])$ — is genuinely infinite-dimensional.

<Callout kind="remark" title="Infinite dimensions are fine here">
For all three named theorems we will prove (Parseval, Fourier inversion, Plancherel), the proof is literally the same algebraic manipulation. The difference is that in infinite dimensions the sums must *converge*, and the convergence is guaranteed because $L^2$ is a Hilbert space with an orthonormal basis of exponentials. Just trust this and move on.
</Callout>
