<SlideLayout title="Fourier analysis on finite abelian groups">

Now let $Z$ be any <Term k="finiteAbelianGroup">finite abelian group</Term>. Functions $Z \to \CC$ form a $|Z|$-dimensional space with the same averaging inner product:

$$\langle f, g \rangle = \frac{1}{|Z|} \sum_{x \in Z} f(x)\,\overline{g(x)}.$$

To define characters we need a way to "multiply" a frequency $\xi$ by a physical point $x$ and land in $\TT$.

<Callout kind="definition" title="Symmetric non-degenerate bilinear form · : Z × Z → 𝕋">
A map $\cdot \colon Z \times Z \to \TT$ that is:

- **Bilinear:** $\xi \cdot (x_1 + x_2) = \xi \cdot x_1 + \xi \cdot x_2$ (and similarly in $\xi$),
- **Symmetric:** $\xi \cdot x = x \cdot \xi$,
- **Non-degenerate:** for any $\xi \neq 0$, there exists $x$ with $\xi \cdot x \neq 0$.
</Callout>

<Callout kind="example" title="The form on ℤ/nℤ">
If $Z = \Zc{n}$, take $\xi \cdot x = \frac{\xi x}{n} \in \TT$. This is bilinear (multiplication distributes), symmetric, and non-degenerate (for $\xi \neq 0$, take $x = 1$).
</Callout>

The choice of $\cdot$ will not matter for our main results — but we need *some* such form to define the characters.
