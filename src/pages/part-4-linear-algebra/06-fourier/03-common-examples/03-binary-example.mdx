<SlideLayout title="Binary Fourier — worked example">

Let $n = 2$. The four characters are $1, x_1, x_2, x_1 x_2$.

<Callout kind="example" title="Fourier decomposition of an indicator function">
Let $f$ be the function that equals $1$ at $(1,1)$ and $0$ elsewhere. We can write

$$f(x_1, x_2) = \frac{x_1+1}{2} \cdot \frac{x_2+1}{2} = \frac{1}{4}(1 + x_1 + x_2 + x_1 x_2).$$

So the Fourier coefficients are $\wh{f}(S) = \frac{1}{4}$ for each of the four subsets $S \subseteq \{1,2\}$.
</Callout>

The empty-set frequency has a special interpretation:

$$\wh{f}(\varnothing) = \langle f, \chi_\varnothing \rangle = \frac{1}{|Z|} \sum_{x \in Z} f(x)$$

which is just the **average value** of $f$ over all inputs.
