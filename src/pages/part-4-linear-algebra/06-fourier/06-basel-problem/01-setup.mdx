<SlideLayout title="Basel problem — setup">

One elegant application of Parseval: computing $\sum_{n \ge 1} \frac{1}{n^2}$.

<Callout kind="theorem" title="Basel problem">
$$\sum_{n \ge 1} \frac{1}{n^2} = \frac{\pi^2}{6}.$$
</Callout>

The strategy: find a function $f \in L^2([-\pi,\pi])$ whose Fourier coefficients involve $\frac{1}{n}$, then apply Parseval to get $\sum_n |\wh{f}(n)|^2$ in closed form.

**The function:** $f(x) = x$ on $[-\pi, \pi]$. This is square-integrable (it's bounded), so $f \in L^2([-\pi,\pi])$.

By Parseval:

$$\sum_{n \in \ZZ} |\wh{f}(n)|^2 = \langle f, f \rangle = \frac{1}{2\pi} \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} x^2\, dx.$$

A direct calculus computation gives:

$$\frac{1}{2\pi} \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} x^2\, dx = \frac{\pi^2}{3}.$$

</SlideLayout>
