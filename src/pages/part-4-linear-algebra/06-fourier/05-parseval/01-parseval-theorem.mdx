<SlideLayout title="Parseval's theorem">

The orthonormal-basis fact $\langle x, x \rangle = \sum_\xi |a_\xi|^2$ translates directly into Fourier language.

<Callout kind="corollary" title="Parseval's theorem">
**Finite abelian group:** For $f \colon Z \to \CC$,

$$\sum_{\xi \in Z} |\wh{f}(\xi)|^2 = \frac{1}{|Z|} \sum_{x \in Z} |f(x)|^2.$$

**Fourier series:** For square-integrable $f \colon [-\pi,\pi] \to \CC$,

$$\sum_{n \in \ZZ} |\wh{f}(n)|^2 = \frac{1}{2\pi} \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} |f(x)|^2\, dx.$$
</Callout>

*Proof:* Both sides equal $\langle f, f \rangle$ (the left by the orthonormal-basis fact; the right by definition of the inner product).

<Callout kind="remark">
<Term k="parsevalThm">Parseval's theorem</Term> says: the "energy" of $f$ in physical space equals the total "energy" spread across all frequencies. No energy is lost or created by the Fourier transform.
</Callout>

</SlideLayout>
