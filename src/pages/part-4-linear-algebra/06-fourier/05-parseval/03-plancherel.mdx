<SlideLayout title="Plancherel's theorem">

The third orthonormal-basis fact $\langle x, y \rangle = \sum_\xi a_\xi \overline{b_\xi}$ gives the most general result.

<Callout kind="corollary" title="Plancherel's theorem">
**Finite abelian group:** For $f, g \colon Z \to \CC$,

$$\langle f, g \rangle = \sum_{\xi \in Z} \wh{f}(\xi)\,\overline{\wh{g}(\xi)}.$$

**Fourier series:** For square-integrable $f, g$,

$$\langle f, g \rangle = \sum_{n \in \ZZ} \wh{f}(n)\,\overline{\wh{g}(n)}.$$
</Callout>

<Callout kind="remark">
<Term k="plancherelThm">Plancherel's theorem</Term> says the Fourier transform preserves inner products — it is a unitary map from the function space to the sequence space $\ell^2(\hat{Z})$. Parseval's theorem is the special case $f = g$.
</Callout>
