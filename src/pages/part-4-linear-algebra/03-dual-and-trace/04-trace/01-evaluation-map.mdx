<SlideLayout title="The evaluation map">

We have a natural linear map from <Term k="dualSpace">$V^\vee$</Term> $\otimes V$ to the ground field $k$.

<Callout kind="definition" title="Evaluation map">
The **evaluation map** $\operatorname{ev} \colon V^\vee \otimes V \to k$ is defined on pure tensors by

$$\operatorname{ev}(\xi \otimes v) = \xi(v)$$

and extended linearly to all sums. It "collapses" a covector–vector pair to the scalar that results from applying the covector to the vector.
</Callout>

On the basis $\{e_i^\vee \otimes e_j\}$ of $V^\vee \otimes V$:

$$\operatorname{ev}(e_i^\vee \otimes e_j) = e_i^\vee(e_j) = \begin{cases} 1 & i = j \\ 0 & i \neq j \end{cases}$$

So the evaluation map picks out exactly the "diagonal" terms.

</SlideLayout>
