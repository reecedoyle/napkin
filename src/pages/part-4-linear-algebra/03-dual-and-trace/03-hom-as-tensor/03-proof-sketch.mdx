<Callout kind="proposition" title="Sketch of proof">
The map $\Psi: V^\vee \otimes W \to \operatorname{Hom}(V,W)$ is an isomorphism. Proof in two steps:

1. **Surjectivity:** Given any $T: V \to W$, write $T(e_i) = w_i$ for each basis element. Then $e_1^\vee \otimes w_1 + \cdots + e_n^\vee \otimes w_n$ maps to $T$ under $\Psi$.

2. **Dimension count:** Both sides have dimension $\dim V \cdot \dim W$, so a surjective map between equal-dimensional spaces is an isomorphism.
</Callout>

The isomorphism $\Psi$ is "natural" — it does not depend on which basis you choose for $V$ or $W$. The *proof* uses coordinates, but the conclusion is coordinate-free.
