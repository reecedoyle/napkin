The <Term k="tensorProduct">tensor product</Term> $V \otimes_k W$ formalises the idea of "multiplying" vectors from two spaces.

<Callout kind="definition" title="Tensor product">
Let $V$ and $W$ be vector spaces over the same field $k$. The **tensor product** $V \otimes_k W$ is the vector space generated by symbols $v \otimes w$ (for $v \in V$, $w \in W$), subject to:
$$
(v_1 + v_2) \otimes w = v_1 \otimes w + v_2 \otimes w
$$
$$
v \otimes (w_1 + w_2) = v \otimes w_1 + v \otimes w_2
$$
$$
(c \cdot v) \otimes w = v \otimes (c \cdot w) = c \cdot (v \otimes w)
$$
</Callout>

An element of the form $v \otimes w$ is called a <Term k="pureTensor">pure tensor</Term>. The symbol $\otimes$ acts as a "wall" through which scalars can pass freely, but which otherwise keeps elements of $V$ and $W$ separated.

General elements of $V \otimes W$ are *sums* of pure tensors — not every element is itself a pure tensor.
