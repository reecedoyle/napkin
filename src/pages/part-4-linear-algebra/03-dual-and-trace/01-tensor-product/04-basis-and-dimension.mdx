The <Term k="tensorProduct">tensor product</Term> delivers the multiplicative dimension count we wanted.

<Callout kind="proposition" title="Basis of V ⊗ W">
Let $V$ and $W$ be finite-dimensional $k$-vector spaces with bases $e_1, \dots, e_m$ and $f_1, \dots, f_n$ respectively. Then
$$\{ e_i \otimes f_j \mid 1 \le i \le m,\; 1 \le j \le n \}$$
is a basis of $V \otimes_k W$. In particular, $\dim(V \otimes W) = \dim V \cdot \dim W$.
</Callout>

**Example:** Let $V$ have basis $e_1, e_2$ and $W$ have basis $f_1, f_2$. To expand $v \otimes w$ where $v = 3e_1 + 4e_2$ and $w = 5f_1 + 6f_2$:

$$v \otimes w = 15(e_1 \otimes f_1) + 20(e_2 \otimes f_1) + 18(e_1 \otimes f_2) + 24(e_2 \otimes f_2)$$
