<SlideLayout title="Pure tensors and scalar-passing">

The key feature of $\otimes$ is that scalars pass through the wall:

$$4x^2 \otimes y = x^2 \otimes 4y = 2x^2 \otimes 2y$$

All three expressions name the same <Term k="pureTensor">pure tensor</Term> in $V \otimes W$. Addition distributes over $\otimes$ in both arguments:

$$(x + 1) \otimes (y + 1) = x \otimes y + 1 \otimes y + x \otimes 1 + 1 \otimes 1$$

<Callout kind="remark" title="Not every element is a pure tensor">
Elements of $V \otimes W$ are **sums** of pure tensors. Just as not every polynomial in $\mathbb{R}[x,y]$ factors as $f(x) \cdot g(y)$, not every element of $V \otimes W$ is a single $v \otimes w$.
</Callout>
