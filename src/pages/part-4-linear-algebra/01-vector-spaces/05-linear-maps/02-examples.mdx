<Callout kind="example" title="Examples of linear maps">

- **Zero map**: $V \to W$ sending everything to $0_W$. Always <Term k="linearMap">linear</Term>.
- **Identity**: $\id \colon V \to V$. A <Term k="linearIso">linear isomorphism</Term>.
- **Projection**: $\RR^3 \to \RR$ by $(a, b, c) \mapsto 4a + 2b + c$. Linear.
- **Evaluation at 3**: $V \to \RR$ by $ax^2 + bx + c \mapsto 9a + 3b + c$, where $V = \{ax^2 + bx + c\}$. Linear.
- **The map** $\RR^3 \to V$ by $(a, b, c) \mapsto ax^2 + bx + c$ is an isomorphism.
- **Multiply by $\sqrt{2}$**: $\QQ[\sqrt{2}] \to \QQ[\sqrt{2}]$ sending $a + b\sqrt{2} \mapsto 2b + a\sqrt{2}$. An isomorphism — its inverse is "multiply by $1/\sqrt{2}$".
</Callout>

<Callout kind="proposition" title="n-dimensional spaces are isomorphic to k^n">
If $V$ is an $n$-dimensional vector space over $k$, then $V \cong k^{\oplus n}$.
</Callout>

The isomorphism: pick any basis $e_1, \dots, e_n$ of $V$, then $a_1 e_1 + \cdots + a_n e_n \mapsto (a_1, \dots, a_n)$.
