  prompt="Let V and W be finite-dimensional vector spaces with nonzero dimension. Complete the table by writing 'always', 'sometimes', or 'never' for each entry: (i) If dim V > dim W, is T injective / surjective / isomorphism? (ii) If dim V = dim W? (iii) If dim V < dim W?"
  hint="Use rank-nullity. Also consider the zero map."
  solution="If dim V > dim W: T injective — never (ker T has positive dimension by rank-nullity); T surjective — sometimes (e.g. projection ℝ² → ℝ, or zero map); T isomorphism — never. If dim V = dim W: injective — sometimes, surjective — sometimes, isomorphism — sometimes (all three are equivalent when they hold). If dim V < dim W: injective — sometimes (e.g. inclusion ℝ → ℝ²); surjective — never (image has dimension ≤ dim V < dim W); isomorphism — never. The 'nevers' all follow from rank-nullity: dim T(V) ≤ min(dim V, dim W)."

<Problem
  client:load
  id="vs-prob-equal-dim-equiv"
  prompt="Let V and W be finite-dimensional with dim V = dim W. Prove that for a linear map T: V → W, the following are equivalent: (i) T is injective, (ii) T is surjective, (iii) T is bijective."
  solution="(iii) → (i) and (iii) → (ii) are immediate. For (i) → (ii): if T is injective then ker T = {0}, so dim T(V) = dim V − 0 = dim V = dim W by rank-nullity, hence T(V) = W. For (ii) → (i): if T is surjective then dim T(V) = dim W = dim V, so dim ker T = dim V − dim T(V) = 0, hence ker T = {0}."
  difficulty="daggered"
/>

<Problem

  prompt="A magic square is a 3×3 matrix of real numbers where every row, column, and both diagonals sum to the same value. Find the dimension of the space of magic squares as a real vector space."
  solution="The space of 3×3 real matrices has dimension 9. A magic square is determined by linear constraints: all row/column/diagonal sums equal some common value s. One can check that the space of magic squares is 3-dimensional: one basis is the all-ones matrix, and two linearly independent 'traceless' magic squares. (Alternatively, map the space of all 3×3 matrices to ℝ⁸ by (row1 sum − diag sum, row2 sum − diag sum, …), and compute the kernel dimension.)"

<Problem

  prompt="Let V = ℚ[√5] = {a + b√5} be a two-dimensional ℚ-vector space with basis {1, √5}. Write down the 2×2 rational matrix corresponding to multiplication by √5."
  hint="1 ↦ √5 and √5 ↦ 5."
  solution="The map m_{√5}: a + b√5 ↦ √5·(a + b√5) = a√5 + 5b = 5b + a√5. In the basis {1, √5}: 1 ↦ 0·1 + 1·√5 (column (0,1)) and √5 ↦ 5·1 + 0·√5 (column (5,0)). So the matrix is [[0, 5],[1, 0]]."
