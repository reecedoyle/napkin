<Callout kind="definition" title="Geometric and algebraic multiplicity">
Let $T \colon V \to V$ be a linear map and $\lambda$ a scalar.

- The <Term k="geomMult">**geometric multiplicity**</Term> of $\lambda$ is $\dim V_\lambda$ — the dimension of the $\lambda$-eigenspace.

- The <Term k="genEigenspace">**generalised eigenspace**</Term> $V^\lambda$ consists of all $v \in V$ for which $(T - \lambda \id)^n(v) = 0$ for some $n \geq 1$.

- The <Term k="algMult">**algebraic multiplicity**</Term> of $\lambda$ is $\dim V^\lambda$.
</Callout>

We allow "multiplicity zero" when $\lambda$ is not an eigenvalue at all.

In practice: **count the Jordan blocks.**

<Callout kind="proposition" title="Multiplicity via Jordan blocks">
In Jordan form, for an eigenvalue $\lambda$:
- **Geometric multiplicity** = number of Jordan blocks with eigenvalue $\lambda$ (one basis eigenvector per block).
- **Algebraic multiplicity** = sum of the sizes of those Jordan blocks (total diagonal entries for $\lambda$).
</Callout>

<Callout kind="remark">
Geometric multiplicity ≤ algebraic multiplicity always holds. Equality holds for every $\lambda$ if and only if $T$ is <Term k="diagonalizable">**diagonalisable**</Term>.
</Callout>
