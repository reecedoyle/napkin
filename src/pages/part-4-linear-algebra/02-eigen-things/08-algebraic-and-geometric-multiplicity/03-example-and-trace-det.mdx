<Callout kind="example" title="Reading multiplicities from Jordan form">
For the matrix from the previous slide with eigenvalue $\lambda = 7$:

- Geometric multiplicity of $7$ = **2** (two Jordan blocks: one $2 \times 2$ and one $3 \times 3$).
- Algebraic multiplicity of $7$ = **5** (block sizes $2 + 3 = 5$).
- Geometric multiplicity of $9$ = **1**, algebraic multiplicity of $9$ = **1**.
</Callout>

**Trace and determinant preview.** The algebraic multiplicities give us a tentative connection to classical invariants:

- The **trace** of $T$ is the sum of all eigenvalues counted with algebraic multiplicity.
- The **determinant** of $T$ is the product of all eigenvalues counted with algebraic multiplicity.

These definitions require the ground field to be algebraically closed. The next chapters will give coordinate-free (and computationally friendlier) definitions via the characteristic polynomial.
