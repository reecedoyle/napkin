<SlideLayout title="Two notions of &quot;repeated&quot; eigenvalue">

Consider the matrix in Jordan form:

$$
T =
\begin{bmatrix}
7 & 1 & & & & \\
0 & 7 & & & & \\
& & 9 & & & \\
& & & 7 & 1 & 0 \\
& & & 0 & 7 & 1 \\
& & & 0 & 0 & 7
\end{bmatrix}.
$$

Focus on eigenvalue $7$. In what sense is it "repeated"?

- **Algebraic sense:** $7$ appears **five** times on the diagonal.
- **Geometric sense:** There are only **two** independent eigenvectors with eigenvalue $7$: $e_1$ and $e_4$. The vector $e_2$ satisfies $T(e_2) = 7e_2 + e_1$, so it is not an eigenvector.

Both notions are valid and useful. They coincide exactly when every Jordan block is $1 \times 1$ — i.e., when the map is diagonalisable.
