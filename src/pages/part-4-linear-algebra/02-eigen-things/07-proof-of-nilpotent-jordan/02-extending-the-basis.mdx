<SlideLayout title="Proof of Nilpotent Jordan — extending the basis">

Continuing from the previous slide. We have a staircase basis $\mathcal{B}'$ for $W = T(V)$ with $\ell$ staircases. Exactly $\ell$ elements of $\mathcal{B}'$ lie in $\ker T$ (the last element of each staircase). Extend these $\ell$ elements to a basis of $\ker T$ by adding vectors $v_{\ell+1}, \dots, v_m$ where $m = \dim \ker T$.

Now form the extended collection

$$
\mathcal{B} = \bigl\{ v_1, T(v_1), T^2(v_1), \dots;\; v_2, T(v_2), \dots;\; \dots;\; v_\ell, T(v_\ell), \dots;\; v_{\ell+1}, \dots, v_m \bigr\}.
$$

**Dimension check.** The number of elements in $\mathcal{B}$ is $\ell + \dim W + (\dim \ker T - \ell) = \dim W + \dim \ker T = \dim V$ (by rank-nullity). So $\mathcal{B}$ has the right size.

  solution="Suppose there is a linear dependence among the elements of ℬ. Group by staircase. Each staircase for v_i contributes terms c_{i,0}·v_i + c_{i,1}·T(v_i) + ···. Apply T to the entire dependence relation: the leading terms v_i drop out (T applied to the bottom of a staircase is 0), leaving a dependence among elements of W that live in ℬ'. Since $\mathcal{B}$ has $\dim V$ linearly independent elements, it is a basis of $V$. By construction it consists of staircases — completing the proof.
