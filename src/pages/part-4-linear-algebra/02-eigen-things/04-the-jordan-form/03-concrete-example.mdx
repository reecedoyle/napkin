<Callout kind="example" title="Reading eigenvectors from Jordan form">
Let $T \colon k^6 \to k^6$ be given by

$$
T = \begin{bmatrix}
5 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\
0 & 2 & 1 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\
0 & 0 & 2 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\
0 & 0 & 0 & 7 & 0 & 0 \\
0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 3 & 0 \\
0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 3 \\
\end{bmatrix}.
$$

The blocks are: $[5]$, $\begin{bmatrix}2&1\\0&2\end{bmatrix}$, $[7]$, $\begin{bmatrix}3&0\\0&3\end{bmatrix}$.

Reading off eigenvectors: $e_1$ is a $5$-eigenvector, $e_2$ is a $2$-eigenvector, $e_4$ is a $7$-eigenvector, and any $ae_5 + be_6$ is a $3$-eigenvector.

The vector $e_3$ is **not** an eigenvector: $T(e_3) = e_2 + 2e_3$, which is not a scalar multiple of $e_3$.
</Callout>
