<Callout kind="remark" title="Adjoint vs dual map">
For a **real** inner product space, $T^\dagger$ and $T^\vee$ are "the same" once you identify $V \cong V^\vee$
via the inner product. For a **complex** inner product space, $T^\dagger$ conjugates entries while $T^\vee$ does not —
they are genuinely different objects.
</Callout>

The advantage of $T^\dagger$: it lives entirely in the world of inner product spaces, no dual space required.
Its matrix is the <Term k="conjugateTranspose">conjugate transpose</Term> $\overline{A^\top}$ whenever you use an orthonormal basis.
