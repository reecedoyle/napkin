<Callout kind="remark" title="The chain of ideas">
1. The **dual map** $T^\vee$ is defined basis-free by pre-composition; its matrix is the transpose of $T$'s matrix.
2. An **inner product** gives a canonical bijection $V \to V^\vee$ (isomorphism over ℝ).
3. The **adjoint** $T^\dagger$ is the inner-product version of the dual map; its matrix is the conjugate transpose.
4. **Normal maps** commute with their adjoint; the spectral theorem says they are exactly the ones diagonalizable in an orthonormal basis.
5. **Hermitian maps** are a special case; their eigenvalues are all real.
</Callout>
