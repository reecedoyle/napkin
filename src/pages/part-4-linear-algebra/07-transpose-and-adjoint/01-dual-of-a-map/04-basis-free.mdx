<SlideLayout title="The basis-free view of transpose">

Standard linear algebra defines the transpose of a matrix by reflecting entries over the diagonal.
That definition depends on which basis you picked.

The dual map gives a **basis-free** version of the same idea:

<Callout kind="remark" title="Why T∨ is the right definition of transpose">
$T^\vee$ is defined purely in terms of composition — no coordinates required.
When you then choose any basis and write $T$ as a matrix, the matrix of $T^\vee$
automatically comes out as the transpose.
So "the transpose" is really a property of the map, not the matrix.
</Callout>
