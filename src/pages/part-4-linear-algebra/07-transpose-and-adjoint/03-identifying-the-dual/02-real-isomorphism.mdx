<Callout kind="theorem" title="Real inner product spaces: V ≅ V∨">
Let $V$ be a finite-dimensional **real** <Term k="innerProductSpace">inner product space</Term>.
The map
$$
\Phi \colon V \to V^\vee, \qquad v \mapsto \langle -, v \rangle,
$$
is an isomorphism of real vector spaces.
</Callout>

**Proof sketch (injectivity).** Suppose $\langle u, v_1 \rangle = \langle u, v_2 \rangle$ for all $u$.
Then $\langle u, v_1 - v_2 \rangle = 0$ for all $u$.
Taking $u = v_1 - v_2$ and using positive-definiteness gives $v_1 - v_2 = 0$.

**Proof sketch (surjectivity).** Take an <Term k="orthonormalBasis">orthonormal basis</Term> $e_1, \dots, e_n$.
Then $\Phi(e_i) = e_i^\vee$, so $\Phi$ sends the basis to the dual basis — hence is surjective.

<Callout kind="remark">
Since $\dim V = \dim V^\vee$, proving either injectivity or surjectivity is enough;
Evan Chen includes both for elegance.
The moral: if a real inner product space is given, $V$ and $V^\vee$ are canonically isomorphic.
</Callout>

</SlideLayout>
