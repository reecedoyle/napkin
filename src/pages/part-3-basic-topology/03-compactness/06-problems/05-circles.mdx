The centre of K₀ lies on some circle C₁; let K₁ be the closed disk with boundary C₁. Since C₁ passes through the centre of K₀, we have K₁ ⊆ K₀. Continue: Kₙ ⊆ Kₙ₋₁ is a nested sequence of nonempty closed subsets of the compact set K₀. The radii decrease (each Kₙ₊₁ has diameter at most half that of Kₙ), so by Cantor's intersection theorem ∩Kₙ ≠ ∅. But ∩Kₙ is a single point lying on no circle of the partition — contradiction. (b) No. Assume such a partition exists. Colour each circle magenta if it encloses p but not q, cyan if it encloses q but not p, and neon yellow if it encloses both. By the argument of (a) applied to regions containing neither, every point is coloured. One can show the set of magenta points is either a closed disk or open; similarly for cyan. Since ℝ² minus a point or two is connected, neither a closed-disk/open-set pair nor a neon-yellow-enclosing arrangement is consistent with connectivity. A full proof works through cases on whether maximal magenta/cyan circles exist."
