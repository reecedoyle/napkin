<Callout kind="theorem" title="Heine-Borel (general metric spaces)">
For a metric space $M$, the following are equivalent:
1. Every sequence has a convergent subsequence (**sequential compactness**).
2. $M$ is complete and <Term k="totallyBounded">totally bounded</Term>.
3. Every open cover has a finite subcover (**open-cover compactness**).
</Callout>

The equivalence $(1) \iff (2)$ uses the bisection idea from the proof that $[0,1]$ is compact: total boundedness provides the pigeonhole, and completeness ensures the Cauchy sequence found converges.

The harder direction is $(1),(2) \Rightarrow (3)$. The key tool is the **Lebesgue number lemma**.
