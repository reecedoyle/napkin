<SlideLayout title="Clopen sets">

In any topological space, $\varnothing$ and $X$ are both open **and** closed (their complements are $X$ and $\varnothing$ respectively, both open). The interesting question is whether any other sets share this property.

<Callout kind="definition" title="Clopen set">
A subset $S$ of a topological space $X$ is <Term k="clopen">clopen</Term> if it is both open and closed in $X$ — equivalently, both $S$ and $X \setminus S$ are open.
</Callout>

If a nontrivial clopen set exists (one other than $\varnothing$ and $X$), then $X$ splits into two disjoint nonempty open pieces: $S$ and $X \setminus S$. That is, the space is "broken into two parts" with no connection between them.

</SlideLayout>
