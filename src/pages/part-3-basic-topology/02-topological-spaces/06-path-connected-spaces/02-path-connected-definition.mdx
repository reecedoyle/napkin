<SlideLayout title="Path-connected spaces">

<Callout kind="definition" title="Path-connected">
A space $X$ is <Term k="pathConnected">path-connected</Term> if any two points in $X$ are connected by some path — i.e., for any $p, q \in X$ there exists a path $\gamma \colon [0,1] \to X$ with $\gamma(0) = p$ and $\gamma(1) = q$.
</Callout>

<Callout kind="example" title="Path-connected spaces">
- $\RR^2$ is path-connected: connect any two points with a straight-line segment.
- The unit circle $S^1$ is path-connected: connect any two points with a circular arc.
</Callout>

Path-connectedness is strictly stronger than connectedness: every path-connected space is connected, but not every connected space is path-connected (though counterexamples are exotic).
