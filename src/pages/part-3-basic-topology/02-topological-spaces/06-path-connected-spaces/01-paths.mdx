<Callout kind="definition" title="Path">
A <Term k="path">path</Term> in a topological space $X$ is a continuous function
$$\gamma \colon [0,1] \to X.$$
Its <Term k="pathEndpoints">endpoints</Term> are $\gamma(0)$ and $\gamma(1)$.
</Callout>

Think of $[0,1]$ as a time parameter $t \in [0,1]$. A path is a continuous journey through $X$ starting at $\gamma(0)$ and ending at $\gamma(1)$.

This agrees perfectly with intuition: a "path" from point $A$ to point $B$ is something you can walk continuously from $A$ to $B$.
