Two equivalent definitions of "closed" existed in metric spaces. Only one survives in full generality:

<Callout kind="definition" title="Closed set and closure">
A subset $S \subseteq X$ is <Term k="closedSetTop">closed</Term> in $X$ if its complement $X \setminus S$ is open.

The <Term k="closureTop">closure</Term> of $S$, written $\ol{S}$, is the smallest closed set containing $S$.
</Callout>

The closure exists because an arbitrary intersection of closed sets is again closed (check: the complement is then an arbitrary union of open sets, which is open).
