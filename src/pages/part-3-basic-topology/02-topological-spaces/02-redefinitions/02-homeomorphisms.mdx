<Callout kind="definition" title="Homeomorphism">
A <Term k="homeomorphismTop">homeomorphism</Term> of topological spaces $X$ and $Y$ is a bijection $f \colon X \to Y$ that induces a bijection between their open sets — equivalently, both $f$ and its inverse $f^{-1}$ are continuous.
</Callout>

Any property that can be stated purely in terms of open sets is preserved by homeomorphism. Such a property is called a <Term k="topologicalProperty">topological property</Term>.

Every concept we define in this chapter — Hausdorff, connected, path-connected, simply connected — will be a topological property, because each is defined purely in terms of which subsets are open.
