<Callout kind="definition" title="Hausdorff space">
A topological space $X$ is <Term k="hausdorff">Hausdorff</Term> if for any two distinct points $p, q \in X$ there exist open neighborhoods $U \ni p$ and $V \ni q$ with
$$U \cap V = \varnothing.$$
</Callout>

The picture: draw disjoint "bubbles" around $p$ and $q$. Hausdorff says you can always do this.

Every metric space is Hausdorff: given distinct $p, q$ with $d(p,q) = r > 0$, take the open balls of radius $r/2$ around each — they are disjoint.
