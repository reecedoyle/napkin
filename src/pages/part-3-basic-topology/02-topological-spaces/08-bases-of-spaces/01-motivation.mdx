<SlideLayout title="Why we need bases">

Describing all open sets of $\RR$ is unwieldy: they include things like
$$\left(0,1\right) \cup \left(1,\tfrac32\right) \cup \left(2, \tfrac73\right) \cup (2014, 2015).$$

But this complicated open set is just a union of intervals. In fact, **every** open set in $\RR$ is a union of open intervals.

So rather than cataloguing all open sets, it suffices to specify a small generating family and declare: "take all unions." That generating family is called a **basis**.

</SlideLayout>
