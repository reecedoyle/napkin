<Callout kind="example" title="Three basic topologies">
Given a set $X$:

- **Metric topology.** Any metric space gives a topological space: take $\mathcal{T}$ to be the open sets in the metric sense. The three axioms hold.
- **Discrete topology.** Every subset of $X$ is declared open. This is the topology induced by the <Term k="discreteMetric">discrete metric</Term>.
- **Indiscrete topology.** Only $\varnothing$ and $X$ are open. This is the opposite extreme — it forgets as much structure as possible.
</Callout>

The <Term k="discreteSpace">discrete space</Term> is at one extreme (every set open), the indiscrete topology at the other (only two open sets). Most interesting topologies sit somewhere between.
