<Callout kind="definition" title="Simply connected">
A space $X$ is <Term k="simplyConnected">simply connected</Term> if:
1. $X$ is path-connected, and
2. for any two points $p, q \in X$, all paths from $p$ to $q$ are homotopic.
</Callout>

In $\mathbb{C}$ (or equivalently $\RR^2$), condition 2 holds: any two paths between the same endpoints can be deformed into each other. Hence $\mathbb{C}$ is simply connected. After the meteor strikes, $\mathbb{C} \setminus \{0\}$ fails condition 2, so it is **not** simply connected.

More loosely: a simply connected space has "no holes" — you cannot find a closed loop that wraps around an obstacle. The formal theory is the **fundamental group** $\pi_1(X)$, developed in a later chapter.
