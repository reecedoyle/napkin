<SlideLayout title="Deducing properties from the axioms" part="Part I — Starting Out" chapter="1. Groups · Properties">

In mathematics, a common theme is to require that objects satisfy certain minimalistic properties — keeping particular examples in mind for intuition — but then to *ignore* the examples on paper and try to deduce as much as possible from the properties alone.

If you've seen olympiad-style **functional equations**, the flavour is similar: a single property of a function is sometimes enough to pin down everything about it.

<Callout kind="remark" title="The game we're playing">
We have just three axioms — identity, associativity, inverses. From now on, anything we say about a "group" must be derivable from those three rules. Surprising amounts will fall out.
</Callout>

Let's see what we can squeeze out of the definition.
