A few quick isomorphisms to build intuition.

<Callout kind="example" title="Examples of isomorphisms">
Let $G$ and $H$ be groups.

- **(a)** $\ZZ \cong 10\ZZ$, via $x \mapsto 10x$, as on the previous slide.
- **(b)** Swapping coordinates gives an isomorphism
$$
G \times H \cong H \times G, \qquad (g, h) \mapsto (h, g).
$$
- **(c)** The identity map $\id \colon G \to G$ is always an isomorphism, so $G \cong G$.
- **(d)** Negation gives a *different* self-isomorphism of $\ZZ$: the map $x \mapsto -x$ is a bijection $\ZZ \to \ZZ$ that respects addition, since $-(x + y) = (-x) + (-y)$.
</Callout>

Examples (c) and (d) together show something interesting: a group can be isomorphic to itself by more than one map. The identity is the obvious choice, but it's rarely the only one.
