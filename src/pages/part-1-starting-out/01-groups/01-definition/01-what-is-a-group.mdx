<SlideLayout title="What is a group?">

A group is one of the most basic structures in higher mathematics. In this chapter we'll cover only the bare minimum: what a group is, and when two groups are the same.

A group consists of two pieces of data:

- a set $G$, and
- an associative binary operation <Term k="star">$\star$</Term> on $G$ with some properties.

Before we write down the formal definition, let's look at two examples to build intuition.

<Callout kind="remark" title="Prototypical examples">
Keep in mind the additive group <Term k="ZZ">$(\ZZ, +)$</Term> and the cyclic group <Term k="Zc">$\Zc{n}$</Term>. But don't forget that most groups are *non-commutative* — that's where the subject gets interesting.
</Callout>
