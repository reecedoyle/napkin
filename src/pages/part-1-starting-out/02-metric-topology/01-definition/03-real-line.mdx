<SlideLayout title="Metric spaces on the real line">

The most familiar example: the real line, with the usual notion of distance.

<Callout kind="example" title="Metric spaces of R">

- The real line <Term k="RR">$\RR$</Term> is a metric space under the metric
$$
d(x, y) = |x - y|.
$$
- The closed interval $[0, 1]$ is also a metric space, with the same distance function.
- In fact, *any* subset $S \subseteq \RR$ inherits a metric this way — just restrict $d$ to pairs of points in $S$.

</Callout>

This *inheritance* trick (a subset of a metric space is again a metric space) is one we will use over and over. Whenever you fish out a subset, you get a metric space "for free".
