<Callout kind="definition" title="Metric space">
A <Term k="metricSpace">**metric space**</Term> is a pair $(M, d)$ consisting of a set of points $M$ together with a <Term k="metric">**metric**</Term> $d \colon M \times M \to \RR_{\ge 0}$. The distance function must obey:

- **Symmetry.** For any $x, y \in M$,
$$
d(x, y) = d(y, x).
$$
- <Term k="positiveDef">**Positive-definiteness.**</Term> $d(x, y) \ge 0$, with equality if and only if $x = y$.
- The <Term k="triangleIneq">**triangle inequality**</Term>: for all $x, y, z \in M$,
$$
d(x, z) + d(z, y) \ge d(x, y).
$$
</Callout>

<Callout kind="remark" title="Notation">
Just like with groups, we will usually abbreviate $(M, d)$ as just $M$, leaving the metric implicit.
</Callout>
