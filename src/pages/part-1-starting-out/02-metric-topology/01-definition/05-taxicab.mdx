The Euclidean metric isn't the only sensible distance on $\RR^2$. Here is a second, very different one.

<Callout kind="example" title="Taxicab on R^2">
The <Term k="taxicabMetric">**taxicab distance**</Term> on $\RR^2$ is
$$
d\bigl( (x_1, y_1), (x_2, y_2) \bigr) = |x_1 - x_2| + |y_1 - y_2|.
$$
It is the path length you would travel between two points on a city grid, where you can only walk along the axis-aligned streets.
</Callout>

Two points can be 5 apart in the Euclidean metric but 7 apart in the taxicab metric — the same set of points carries two genuinely different geometries. (For most of this chapter we will default back to the more natural Euclidean metric.)
