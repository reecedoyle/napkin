Moving up a dimension: the plane, with straight-line distance.

<Callout kind="example" title="Metric spaces of R^2">

- We make <Term k="RRn">$\RR^2$</Term> a metric space by imposing the <Term k="euclideanMetric">**Euclidean distance**</Term>
$$
d\bigl( (x_1, y_1), (x_2, y_2) \bigr) = \sqrt{(x_1 - x_2)^2 + (y_1 - y_2)^2}.
$$
- Just like for the real line, any subset of $\RR^2$ becomes a metric space by inheriting this distance. The unit disk, the unit circle, and the unit square $[0,1]^2$ are special cases.

</Callout>

The Euclidean metric on $\RR^2$ is our running prototype for the whole chapter. When you read "metric space", picture this first.
