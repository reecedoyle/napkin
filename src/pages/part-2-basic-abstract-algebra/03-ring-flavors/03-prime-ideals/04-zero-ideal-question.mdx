<SlideLayout title="When is (0) a prime ideal?">

The quotient theorem lets us answer a small puzzle: what is special about a ring where the zero ideal is prime?

If $(0)$ is prime, then $R/(0) \cong R$ is an integral domain. Conversely, if $R$ is an integral domain, $(0)$ is prime. So:

<Callout kind="proposition">
The zero ideal $(0)$ is prime in $R$ if and only if $R$ is an integral domain.
</Callout>
