  prompt="(Krull's theorem) Let R be a ring and J a proper ideal.
(a) Prove that if R is Noetherian, then J is contained in a maximal ideal.
(b) Use Zorn's lemma to prove the result even if R is not Noetherian."
  hint="Keep adding elements to get an ascending chain. For (b), take the poset of proper ideals containing J and apply Zorn's lemma — verify the union of a chain of proper ideals is proper."
  solution="(a) Noetherian case: start with J. If J is maximal, done. Otherwise, J is properly contained in some ideal J_1. If J_1 is maximal, done; otherwise it's contained in J_2. The Noetherian condition says there are no infinite strictly ascending chains of ideals, so this process terminates — at a maximal ideal containing J.

(b) Zorn's lemma: let P be the poset of proper ideals of R that contain J, ordered by inclusion. P is nonempty (J ∈ P). For any chain (totally ordered subset) in P, take its union U = ∪I_α. U is an ideal (routine to check), U ⊇ J, and U is proper: if 1 ∈ U then 1 ∈ I_α for some α, contradicting I_α being proper. So U ∈ P is an upper bound for the chain. By Zorn's lemma, P has a maximal element M. This M is a proper ideal containing J, and it is maximal in R: any ideal properly containing M would also be in P and properly contain M, contradicting maximality of M in P."
  difficulty="starred"

  prompt="Describe all prime ideals of ℂ[x] and ℝ[x]."
  hint="Both rings are PIDs. In a PID, every nonzero prime ideal is maximal and generated by an irreducible element."
  solution="Both ℂ[x] and ℝ[x] are PIDs (Euclidean domains under degree). In a PID, nonzero prime ideals are exactly (p) for irreducible p.

For ℂ[x]: by the fundamental theorem of algebra, every polynomial over ℂ factors into linear factors. So the irreducibles are exactly (x - z) for z ∈ ℂ. The prime ideals are (0) and (x - z) for each z ∈ ℂ.

For ℝ[x]: the irreducible polynomials over ℝ are linear factors (x - a) for a ∈ ℝ, and quadratics (x² - ax + b) with negative discriminant (a² - 4b < 0), i.e. those with two conjugate non-real complex roots. The prime ideals are (0), (x - a) for a ∈ ℝ, and (x² - ax + b) for each such irreducible quadratic."
