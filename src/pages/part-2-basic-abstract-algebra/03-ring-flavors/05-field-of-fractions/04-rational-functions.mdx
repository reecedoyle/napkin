The field of fractions of a polynomial ring is particularly useful in algebra and analysis.

<Callout kind="definition" title="Field of rational functions">
Let $k$ be a field. We define the **field of rational functions**:
$$k(x) = \operatorname{Frac}(k[x])$$
Elements of $k(x)$ are ratios $f(x)/g(x)$ where $f, g \in k[x]$ and $g \neq 0$, identified up to common polynomial factors.
</Callout>

For example, $\mathbb{Q}(x)$ contains expressions like $\dfrac{x^2 + 1}{x - 3}$ and $\dfrac{1}{x^2 + x + 1}$. The "variables" here are formal — we're not plugging in numbers.

This is analogous to how $\mathbb{Q}$ "contains" $\mathbb{Z}$ by embedding $n \mapsto n/1$; similarly $k[x] \hookrightarrow k(x)$ by $f \mapsto f/1$.
