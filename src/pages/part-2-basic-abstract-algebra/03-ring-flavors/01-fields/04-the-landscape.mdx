Before diving in, here is the full table of correspondences this chapter builds toward. Each row pairs a "niceness" property of a ring with the corresponding property of its ideals.

| Ring noun | Ideal adjective | Connection |
|---|---|---|
| Field | maximal | $R/I$ is a field $\iff$ $I$ is maximal |
| Integral domain | prime | $R/I$ is an integral domain $\iff$ $I$ is prime |
| PID | principal | $R$ is a PID $\iff$ $R$ is an integral domain and every $I$ is principal |
| Noetherian ring | finitely generated | $R$ is Noetherian $\iff$ every $I$ is finitely generated |

<Callout kind="remark">
Think of this table as the chapter's **road map**. We'll fill in each row over the next several sections, with fields and integral domains first, then principal ideal domains and unique factorization.
</Callout>

Each ring noun on the left is stronger than the one below it: every field is an integral domain, and every integral domain with all ideals principal is a PID.
