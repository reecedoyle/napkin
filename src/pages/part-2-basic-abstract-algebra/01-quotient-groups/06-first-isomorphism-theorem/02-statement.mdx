<Callout kind="theorem" title="First isomorphism theorem">
Let $\phi \colon G \to H$ be a group homomorphism. Then

$$
G / \ker\phi \;\cong\; \phi(G).
$$

That is, the quotient of $G$ by the kernel of $\phi$ is isomorphic to the image of $\phi$.
</Callout>

The isomorphism is given by the well-defined map $\widetilde\phi: G/\ker\phi \to \phi(G)$ defined by $\widetilde\phi(g\ker\phi) = \phi(g)$. This map is surjective by definition of $\phi(G)$, and injective because $\widetilde\phi(g\ker\phi) = 1_H \iff g \in \ker\phi \iff g\ker\phi = 1_{G/\ker\phi}$.

<Callout kind="remark" title="Reformulation">
Every surjective homomorphism $\phi: G \twoheadrightarrow Q$ is "the same" as the canonical quotient map $G \twoheadrightarrow G/\ker\phi$, followed by an isomorphism $G/\ker\phi \xrightarrow{\sim} Q$.
</Callout>
