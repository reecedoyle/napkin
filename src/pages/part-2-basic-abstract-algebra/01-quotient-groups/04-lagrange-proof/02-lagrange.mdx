<SlideLayout title="Lagrange's theorem">

<Callout kind="theorem" title="Lagrange's theorem">
Let $G$ be a finite group and $H$ any subgroup of $G$. Then $|H|$ divides $|G|$.
</Callout>

**Proof:** The cosets of $H$ partition $G$ into equally-sized pieces, each of size $|H|$. If there are $n$ cosets, then:

$$
|G| = n \cdot |H|.
$$

Hence $|H|$ divides $|G|$. $\square$

The number $n = |G|/|H|$ is called the <Term k="indexSubgroup">**index**</Term> of $H$ in $G$, written $[G:H]$.

<Callout kind="corollary" title="Order of an element divides |G|">
For any $g \in G$, the subgroup $\langle g \rangle$ has order $\mathrm{ord}(g)$. Lagrange gives $\mathrm{ord}(g) \mid |G|$, so $g^{|G|} = 1$ for all $g \in G$.
</Callout>

<Callout kind="remark" title="Quotient order formula">
When $N \trianglelefteq G$, the quotient group satisfies $|G/N| = |G|/|N|$ — the number of cosets is the index $[G:N]$.
</Callout>

</SlideLayout>
