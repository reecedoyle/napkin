<Problem
  client:load
  id="quot-prob-s4-order3"
  prompt="Does S₄ have a normal subgroup of order 3?"
  hint="A subgroup of order 3 must be cyclic, generated by a 3-cycle. Can you find a conjugate that lands outside it?"
  solution="No. Any subgroup of order 3 is generated by a 3-cycle (since 3 is prime). By relabelling, assume H = ⟨(1 2 3)⟩. Take g = (3 4). Then g(1 2 3)g⁻¹ = (1 2 4), which is not in H. So H is not closed under conjugation by g, hence not normal."
  difficulty="standard"
/>

<Problem
  client:load
  id="quot-prob-trivial-hom"
  prompt="Let G and H be finite groups with |G| = 1000 and |H| = 999. Show that any homomorphism φ: G → H must be the trivial homomorphism (sending everything to 1_H)."
  hint="Use the first isomorphism theorem: G/ker φ ≅ φ(G). Compare orders using the fact that gcd(1000, 999) = 1."
  solution="By the first isomorphism theorem, G/ker φ ≅ φ(G), a subgroup of H. So |G/ker φ| divides |H| = 999, and |G/ker φ| also divides |G| = 1000. Since gcd(1000, 999) = 1, we must have |G/ker φ| = 1. Hence G/ker φ is the trivial group, so ker φ = G, meaning φ sends everything to 1_H."
  difficulty="standard"
/>
