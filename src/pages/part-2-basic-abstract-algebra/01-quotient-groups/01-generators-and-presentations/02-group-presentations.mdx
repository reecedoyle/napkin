<SlideLayout title="Group presentations">

Listing generators alone is not enough. Consider <Term k="Zc">$\mathbb{Z}/100\mathbb{Z}$</Term>: it is generated by a single element $x$, but $x$ satisfies the extra condition $x^{100} = 1$. Without that, $\langle x \rangle$ could just as well be $\mathbb{Z}$.

The fix: list the generators **and** the <Term k="relations">**relations**</Term> they must satisfy. Two elements are equal if and only if you can get from one to the other by applying those relations. The resulting expression is a <Term k="presentation">**group presentation**</Term>.

$$
\mathbb{Z}/100\mathbb{Z} = \langle x \mid x^{100} = 1 \rangle
$$

<Callout kind="example" title="Dihedral group D₂ₙ">
The dihedral group of order $2n$ has two generators and two relations:

$$
D_{2n} = \langle r, s \mid r^n = s^2 = 1,\; rs = sr^{-1} \rangle.
$$

Every element of $D_{2n}$ can be written uniquely as $r^\alpha$ or $sr^\alpha$ for $\alpha \in \{0, 1, \dots, n-1\}$.
</Callout>

<Callout kind="example" title="Klein four group">
The Klein four group, isomorphic to $\mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z} \times \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$, is

$$
\langle a, b \mid a^2 = b^2 = 1,\; ab = ba \rangle.
$$
</Callout>
