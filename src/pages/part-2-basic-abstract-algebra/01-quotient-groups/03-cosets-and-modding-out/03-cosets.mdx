<SlideLayout title="Left cosets" part="Part II — Basic Abstract Algebra" chapter="3. Quotient groups · Cosets and modding out">

The fibers in the previous slide all have the form $g + N$ (for additive $\mathbb{Z}$). In a general group, they take the form $gN$.

<Callout kind="definition" title="Left coset">
Let $H$ be any subgroup of $G$ and $g \in G$. The <Term k="leftCoset">**left coset**</Term> of $H$ at $g$ is

$$
gH \;\coloneqq\; \{ gh : h \in H \}.
$$
</Callout>

<Callout kind="remark" title="Cosets are sets, not elements">
Two different elements $g_1 \neq g_2$ can give the **same** coset: $g_1 H = g_2 H$ whenever $g_1^{-1} g_2 \in H$. In the mod-100 example, $3 + N = 103 + N = -97 + N$.

If I say "let $gH$ be a coset" without specifying $g$, you cannot recover $g$ from the coset alone. The notation $gH$ refers to a **set**, and that set has many equally valid representatives.
</Callout>

<Callout kind="proposition" title="All cosets have equal size">
Given two cosets $g_1 H$ and $g_2 H$, the map $x \mapsto g_2 g_1^{-1} x$ is a bijection between them. So every coset of $H$ has exactly $|H|$ elements.
</Callout>

</SlideLayout>
