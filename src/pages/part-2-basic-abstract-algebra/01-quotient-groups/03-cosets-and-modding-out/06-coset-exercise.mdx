<SlideLayout title="Working with cosets">

Let's make this concrete. Consider $G = \mathbb{Z}$ and $N = 5\mathbb{Z}$ (the multiples of 5).

The cosets of $N$ in $G$ are:
$$
0+5\mathbb{Z},\quad 1+5\mathbb{Z},\quad 2+5\mathbb{Z},\quad 3+5\mathbb{Z},\quad 4+5\mathbb{Z}.
$$

The quotient $\mathbb{Z}/5\mathbb{Z} = \mathbb{Z}/5\mathbb{Z}$ has exactly 5 elements.

<Callout kind="example" title="Coset arithmetic">
$(2 + 5\mathbb{Z}) + (4 + 5\mathbb{Z}) = (2+4) + 5\mathbb{Z} = 6 + 5\mathbb{Z} = 1 + 5\mathbb{Z}$.

This is just addition mod 5: $2 + 4 \equiv 1 \pmod{5}$.
</Callout>
