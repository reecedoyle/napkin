<SlideLayout title="An explicit quotient computation">

Let's see the quotient construction in a non-integer example.

<Callout kind="example" title="D₈ mod a normal subgroup">
Let $\phi: D_8 \to \mathbb{Z}/4\mathbb{Z}$ be defined by $r \mapsto \overline{2}$, $s \mapsto \overline{2}$.

Computing images of all 8 elements of $D_8 = \{1, r, r^2, r^3, s, sr, sr^2, sr^3\}$:

$$
\phi(1) = \phi(r^2) = \phi(sr) = \phi(sr^3) = \overline{0}
$$
$$
\phi(r) = \phi(r^3) = \phi(s) = \phi(sr^2) = \overline{2}.
$$

So $N = \ker\phi = \{1, r^2, sr, sr^3\}$. The two cosets of $N$ are $N$ and $rN = \{r, r^3, s, sr^2\}$.

Thus $|D_8/N| = 2$, so $D_8/N \cong \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$.
</Callout>

<Callout kind="example" title="Product group quotient">
In $G \times H$, the subgroup $G' = \{(g, 1_H) : g \in G\} \cong G$ is normal (easy check). The quotient satisfies $(G \times H)/G' \cong H$: every coset $\{(g, h) : g \in G\}$ is characterised by its $H$-coordinate.
</Callout>
