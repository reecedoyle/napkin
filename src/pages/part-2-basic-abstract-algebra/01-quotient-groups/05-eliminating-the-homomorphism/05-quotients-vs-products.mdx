title: Quotients and products don't cancel
---

<SlideLayout title="Quotients and products don't cancel">

A natural question: if $N \trianglelefteq G$ and we know $G/N$, can we recover $G$ as $(G/N) \times N$?

<Callout kind="example" title="Quotients and products don't cancel">
Take $G = \mathbb{Z}/4\mathbb{Z}$ and $N = \{0, 2\} \cong \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$.

Then $G/N \cong \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$ — so $G/N \cong N$ and both have order 2.

But $G = \mathbb{Z}/4\mathbb{Z}$ is **not** isomorphic to $\mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z} \times \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$. (The latter has no element of order 4; $\mathbb{Z}/4\mathbb{Z}$ does.)

So $(G/N) \times N \not\cong G$ in general.
</Callout>

<Callout kind="remark" title="Extensions are hard">
The problem of reconstructing $G$ from $N$ and $G/N$ is called the **group extension problem**. It is genuinely hard and not fully solved in general — knowing the "layers" of a group does not uniquely determine the group.
</Callout>

</SlideLayout>
