<Callout kind="theorem" title="Algebraic characterisation of normal subgroups">
Let $H$ be a subgroup of $G$. The following are equivalent:
1. $H \trianglelefteq G$ (i.e., $H$ is the kernel of some homomorphism).
2. For every $g \in G$ and $h \in H$, the <Term k="conjugate">**conjugate**</Term> $ghg^{-1} \in H$.
</Callout>

We already proved $(1) \Rightarrow (2)$. For $(2) \Rightarrow (1)$: assume the conjugation condition. We **build** a homomorphism with kernel $H$ — namely the quotient map $g \mapsto gH$ from $G$ to the coset space $G/H$.

The conjugation condition is exactly what makes coset multiplication well-defined. Suppose $g_1' = g_1 h_1$ and $g_2' = g_2 h_2$. We need $g_1' g_2' \sim_H g_1 g_2$:

$$
g_1 h_1 g_2 h_2 = g_1 g_2 (g_2^{-1} h_1 g_2) h_2.
$$

Since $g_2^{-1} h_1 g_2 \in H$ (conjugation condition!), the right side is $g_1 g_2$ times an element of $H$. So $g_1' g_2' \in g_1 g_2 H$.
