We now have a clean, $\phi$-free characterisation of normality.

<Callout kind="theorem" title="Equivalent conditions for N ⊴ G">
Let $N$ be a subgroup of $G$. The following are equivalent:
1. $N$ is the kernel of some homomorphism $G \to H$.
2. For all $g \in G$ and $n \in N$: $gng^{-1} \in N$.
3. The coset multiplication $(g_1 N)(g_2 N) = (g_1 g_2)N$ is well-defined, making $G/N$ a group.
4. The canonical map $\pi: G \to G/N$, $g \mapsto gN$, is a surjective homomorphism with kernel $N$.
</Callout>

Conditions (2)–(4) require no external $\phi$ at all. They are the **intrinsic** definition most textbooks use.
