<Callout kind="definition" title="Zero ring">
The **zero ring** is the ring with a single element, where <Term k="zeroR">$0_R$</Term> $=$ <Term k="oneR">$1_R$</Term>. A ring is **nontrivial** if $0_R \neq 1_R$.
</Callout>

<Callout kind="remark" title="A characterisation of nontriviality">
A ring is nontrivial if and only if $0_R \neq 1_R$. In the zero ring, the single element is forced to serve as both identities. But in any nontrivial ring, the two identities must be distinct — adding the identity to itself gives the identity, which forces $0 + 0 = 0 \neq 1$.
</Callout>

Two key facts that hold in every ring, following from the axioms alone:

$$r \cdot 0_R = 0_R \quad \text{and} \quad r \cdot (-1_R) = -r.$$
