<Callout kind="definition" title="Ring">
A **ring** is a triple <Term k="ring">$(R, +, \times)$</Term> such that:

1. $(R, +)$ is an abelian group, with identity <Term k="zeroR">$0_R$</Term>.
2. $\times$ is an associative binary operation on $R$ with identity <Term k="oneR">$1_R$</Term>.
3. Multiplication distributes over addition: $a(b+c) = ab + ac$.

The ring is **commutative** if $\times$ is commutative. In this chapter, all rings are commutative.
</Callout>

<Callout kind="remark" title="Convention: rings have 1">
Some authors allow rings without a multiplicative identity. We do not — "ring" always means "ring with $1$".
</Callout>
