<Callout kind="example" title="Standard rings">
- <Term k="ZZ">$\mathbb{Z}$</Term>, <Term k="QQ">$\mathbb{Q}$</Term>, <Term k="RR">$\mathbb{R}$</Term>, <Term k="CC">$\mathbb{C}$</Term> are all rings under the usual $+$ and $\times$.
- <Term k="Zc">$\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}$</Term> — the integers mod $n$ — is a ring for every $n \geq 1$.
</Callout>

<Callout kind="definition" title="Zero ring and nontriviality">
The **zero ring** is the ring with a single element (where $0_R = 1_R$). A ring is **nontrivial** when $0_R \neq 1_R$.
</Callout>

A basic but useful fact: for any element $r$ in any ring $R$,

$$r \cdot 0_R = 0_R \quad \text{and} \quad r \cdot (-1_R) = -r.$$

These follow directly from the distributive law — no extra axioms needed.
