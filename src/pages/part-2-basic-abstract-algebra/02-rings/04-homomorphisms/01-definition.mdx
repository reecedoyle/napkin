<Callout kind="definition" title="Ring homomorphism">
Let $R$ and $S$ be rings. A **ring homomorphism** is a map <Term k="ringHom">$\phi \colon R \to S$</Term> such that for all $x, y \in R$:

1. $\phi(x + y) = \phi(x) + \phi(y)$ — respects addition,
2. $\phi(x \cdot y) = \phi(x) \cdot \phi(y)$ — respects multiplication,
3. $\phi(1_R) = 1_S$ — sends identity to identity.

If $\phi$ is also a bijection, it is a **ring isomorphism** and we write $R \cong S$.
</Callout>

The third condition is not redundant. The map $\mathbb{Z} \to \mathbb{Z}$ sending everything to $0$ satisfies (1) and (2) but not (3). We insist on it to rule out such degenerate maps.
